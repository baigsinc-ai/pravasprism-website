Documents Required

Standard checklist for Indian passport holders. Individual cases may vary.

📌 Mandatory
  • Valid passport — 6+ months validity, 2 blank pages
  • DS-160 confirmation page (printed)
  • Visa fee payment receipt ($185)
  • Interview appointment confirmation letter
  • Passport photo — 2"×2", white background
📎 Supporting Documents
  • Bank statements — last 6 months
  • Income Tax Returns — last 3 years
  • Employment letter / business registration
  • Property or fixed asset documents
  • Travel itinerary (flight + hotel)
  • Invitation letter if visiting family/friends
  • Old passports with prior travel history

Total timeline: DS-160 (1–2 days) + appointment wait (currently 400+ days at most consulates — this is the main bottleneck) + post-interview processing (3–15 days). Plan 12–18 months ahead if possible. We help compress the appointment wait significantly.

Yes. A 214(b) refusal — the most common — is not a ban. You can reapply immediately. But reapplying with the same profile almost always means another rejection. You need to address the specific gap that caused the refusal. That's what our rejection recovery service does.

There is no fixed minimum. Officers assess your ability to fund the trip and your ties to India — income, employment, family, property. The amount varies by trip duration and purpose. A stable income and consistent banking history matter more than a one-time large deposit.

DS-160 is the mandatory online non-immigrant visa application form by the US State Department. It collects your personal, travel, and employment details. Errors in this form are a leading cause of complications — we handle this as part of every full filing service.

Yes. The US has consulates in Mumbai, Delhi, Chennai, Kolkata, and Hyderabad. You can apply at any of them regardless of residence. We often recommend choosing based on appointment availability and approval patterns for your profile type.
